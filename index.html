The problem

An egg costs more than you pay.

Animal suffering, emissions, and dependence — packed into every carton. We're so used to it that we've stopped seeing the price.

01
Animal suffering
A life for an egg.

Behind every egg is a hen we raise, feed, and ultimately send to slaughter.

02
Emissions
Ammonia & fine dust.

Poultry farms are roughly a quarter of Dutch farming's fine dust, and ammonia is its largest gaseous emission.

03
Dependence
Feed from overseas.

Laying hens eat imported soy — the Netherlands is the world's second-largest importer, after China.

Even the most sustainable egg farm — solar-powered, fed on food-industry leftovers — still takes from the earth. It can lighten the load, but it never gives fertility back. A lupin field does.

The solution

One bean.
Three ways to win.

Lupin doesn't just do less harm. It actively gives back. Better for the soil, for biodiversity, and for our independence.

01
Soil
Feeds the ground

Lupin pulls nitrogen straight from the air and stores it in the soil for the next crop.

02
Biodiversity
Feeds the bees

In bloom, a lupin field hums with wild bees and insects, bringing life back to the land.

03
Independence
Grown at home

Lupin grows right here, on home soil. No ocean freight, no imported feed. Sovereign, and independent of geopolitics.
